Beef Wellington

Beef wellington is a very tasty sandwich.

Beef Wellington is a steak dish of English origin, made out of fillet steak coated with pate and duxelles, wrapped in puff pastry, then baked. Some recipes include wrapping the coasted meat in a crepe or parma ham to retain moisture and prevent it from making the pastry soggy

A whole tenderloin may be wrapped and baked, and then sliced for serving, or the tenderloin may be sliced for serving, or the tnderloin may be sliced into individual portions prior to wrapping and baking.

  • 2 and half pounds beef tenderloin
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • Half a cup sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 2 ounces liver pate
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 - 17.5 ounce package frozen puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg yolk, beaten
  • 1 - 10.5 ounce can beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons red wine
  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F. Place beef in a small baking fdis, and spread with 2 tablespoons of softened butter. Bake for 10 to 15 minutes, or until browned. Remove from pan, and allow to cool completely. Reserve pan juices.
  2. Melt 2 tablespoons of butter in a skillet over medium heat. Saute onion and mushrooms in butter for 5 minutes. Remove from heat, and let cool.
  3. Mix together with pate and 2 tablespoons of softened butter, and season with salt and pepper. Spread pate over beef. top with onion and mushroom mixture.
  4. Roll out the puff pastry dough, and place beef in the center. Fold up, and seal all the edges, making sure the seams are not too think. place beef in a 9x13 inch backing dis, cut a few slits in the top of the dough, and brush with egg yolk.
  5. Bake at 450 degrees F for 10 minutes, then reduce heat to 425 degrees F for 10 to 15 more minutes, or until pastry is a rich, golden brown. Set aside, and keep warm.
  6. Place all reserved juices in a small saucepan over high heat. Stirin beef stock and red wine; boil for 10 to 15 minutes, or until slightly reduced. Strain, and serve with beef.
